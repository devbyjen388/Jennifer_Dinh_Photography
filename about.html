About Me

As a child, my father would always take out his cameras during family gatherings, vacations, or milestone events with the intent of preserving memory. When I picked up the camera for the first time, it wasn’t with intention or outcome in mind but only curiosity and play. Now, I photograph to understand. I am drawn to relationships: hands interacting with objects, bodies navigating space, and the quiet choreography of everyday life. I also love exploring color—how it shapes emotion, highlights detail, and shapes story. This portfolio is my way of sharing that work. On this site, I have collections of my favorite photographs that I have taken and organized into different galleries.

In the future, I hope to explore more with film photography. I have tried it in the past, but often received photos that were too dark or overexposed. For now, I am focused on my digital photography skills.

My current camera rotation includes a Fujifilm X-T50 and a Canon PowerShot SD1100 IS Digital ELPH.
